Scientific Names

  • Phyllanthus emblica is the main scientific name.
  • Also called Emblica officinalis.
  • Belongs to family Phyllanthaceae.
  • Known as Amla in Hindi and Amalaki in Sanskrit.
  • Phyllanthus means 'leaf-flower' in Greek.
  • Emblica is derived from 'Amalika'.
  • Tree height: 8–18 meters.
  • Flowers are small and greenish-yellow.
  • Fruit is round and six-lobed.
  • Rich in Vitamin C.
  • Researched globally for medicinal properties.
  • Not related to European gooseberry.
  • Part of Kingdom Plantae, Order Malpighiales.
  • Hardy and drought-resistant.
  • Popular in traditional medicine.
  • Used in many herbal formulations.
  • Helps rejuvenate body tissues.
  • Important in Ayurveda and Unani medicine.
  • Known for antioxidant activities.
  • Tree has spiritual significance.
